About BTS

BTS (Bangtan Sonyeondan), also known as the Bangtan Boys, is a seven-member South Korean boy band formed in Seoul in 2013. The name "BTS" stands for the Korean expression Bangtan Sonyeondan (방탄소년단), literally meaning "Bulletproof Boy Scouts".

BTS debuted on June 13, 2013, with their first single "No More Dream". They quickly gained attention for their powerful performances, socially conscious lyrics, and unique blend of hip-hop with other genres. Under Big Hit Entertainment (now HYBE Corporation), they've grown from a small agency group to global superstars.

Their music often focuses on themes of mental health, the troubles of school-age youth, loss, the journey towards self-love, and individualism. Their work also often references literature and psychological concepts, and includes an alternative universe storyline that continues across multiple music videos and album tracks.

Their Journey & Hardships

2013 - Humble Beginnings

Debuted from small company Big Hit Entertainment with limited resources. Faced criticism for their hip-hop concept in a pop-dominated industry. Performed for small audiences and struggled to gain recognition.

2014-2015 - Early Struggles

Members lived together in a small, cramped dorm. Dealt with financial difficulties and the pressure of Korea's competitive music industry. Faced skepticism about their longevity and artistic direction.

2016 - Breaking Through & Pressure

"Blood Sweat & Tears" brought mainstream success but also intense scrutiny. Members faced mental health challenges from sudden fame. J-hope later revealed he considered leaving during this period due to pressure.

2017 - International Recognition & Expectations

Won Top Social Artist at Billboard Music Awards, breaking Justin Bieber's 6-year streak. Faced increased anti-Kpop sentiment and racist comments internationally. Struggled with the weight of representing K-pop globally.

2018 - UN Speech & Exhaustion

Addressed United Nations about youth self-love campaign while dealing with exhausting world tour schedules. RM spoke about the pressure of being a "model" for youth. Members experienced burnout from non-stop activities.

2020 - Pandemic Challenges & Adaptation

Canceled "Map of the Soul" world tour due to COVID-19, causing significant financial impact. Adapted with online concerts "Bang Bang Con". Released "Dynamite" as their first English single amid debates about artistic integrity.

2022 - Military Service & Hiatus

Announced hiatus for mandatory military service, facing uncertainty about group's future. Dealt with public scrutiny about enlistment timing. Promised to return in 2025, testing fan loyalty during the long break.
